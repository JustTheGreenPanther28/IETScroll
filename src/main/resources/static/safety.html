Zero tolerance

Ragging is a crime,
not a tradition.

⚠️

What counts as ragging

Any act — physical, verbal, online, or indirect — that causes embarrassment, fear, hurt, or shame to a student. Forcing juniors to perform tasks, mocking appearances, group isolation, or sending threatening messages on any platform including IET Scroll is ragging.

⚖️

Legal consequences

Ragging is a criminal offence under UGC Regulations 2009 and punishable under IPC. Penalties include expulsion, suspension, FIR registration, and imprisonment. The institution has zero discretion on this — every report is investigated.

📢

Report it. Always.

Staying silent makes you complicit. If you witness or experience ragging — on campus or on IET Scroll — report it immediately. Contact the Anti-Ragging Committee, hostel wardens, or use the national helpline. Your anonymity is protected.

🚫

On IET Scroll specifically

Any message, listing, or post that constitutes ragging will result in and submission of evidence to authorities. No warnings. No second chances.

Community Safety Standard
Platform principle

On IET Scroll,
everyone is equal.

No batch. No branch hierarchy. No "senior privilege." The moment you log in, you are just a student — same as every other student on this platform.

🎓

No batch hierarchy

Whether you're in your first semester or final year, your account has the same access, same voice, and same standing. A 2024 batch student has zero authority over a 2021 batch student here — and vice versa.

🤝

No "senior-junior" dynamic

Those labels exist outside this platform. Inside IET Scroll, there are no juniors to boss around and no seniors to fear. Post, respond, trade, and help without anyone pulling rank on you.

🏫

No branch superiority

CS, IT, EC, ME, CE — every branch is equally valid. No department gets a louder voice. No "core vs non-core" gatekeeping. Your ideas matter regardless of which lab you spend your evenings in.

⚖️

Same rules, same consequences

The community guidelines apply identically to every user — 1st year or 4th year, day scholar or hosteller. Seniority is not a shield from accountability on this platform.

"You earned your seat at IET through hard work. So did every other student here. Respect that. Nobody's seat is more valuable than someone else's — not on this platform, and frankly, not anywhere."

Privacy by
default

01

Verified ≠ unconditionally trusted

Yes, every user is a verified IET DAVV student. But "verified" does not mean "safe to trust completely." This platform is on our own risk. Use your judgment — you don't owe anyone personal information beyond what the platform requires.

02

Prefer your college email

All communication on IET Scroll is strongly preferred through your official @ietdavv.edu.in email. This creates mutual accountability. Avoid sharing personal phone numbers or WhatsApp with strangers on the platform.

03

Share the minimum needed

You don't need to reveal your hostel room, home address, daily schedule, or personal finances to use this platform. Oversharing is a privacy risk — even in a closed, verified community.

04

Real interactions: your responsibility

IET Scroll provides the platform — it does not supervise every conversation or transaction. Real-world meetups, payments, and handoffs are at your own risk. Meet in public campus spaces. Bring a friend if unsure.

Community standards

How we treat
each other here

  • R01

    No harassment of any kind

    This includes juniors, seniors, any gender, any branch. Targeted messages, public mockery, or coordinated pile-ons are immediate ban offences. IET Scroll is not a place to settle personal scores.

    Zero Tolerance
  • R02

    Communicate through official channels

    Whenever possible, use your @ietdavv.edu.in email for transactions, queries, and follow-ups from the platform. This protects you and the other person equally — it's a trail you both control.

    Strongly Recommended
  • R03

    No fake listings or misleading posts

    Lost & Found and Marketplace listings must be accurate and honest. Fake posts waste everyone's time and erode trust. Repeated offences result in permanent account suspension.

    Platform Policy
  • R04

    Don't share your login credentials

    Your IET Scroll account is tied to your verified college identity. Never share your login with anyone, even close friends. Account sharing compromises the integrity of the entire verified community.

    Security Rule
  • R05

    Real-world meetups: stay safe

    When meeting someone from the platform — marketplace handoffs, study group coordination — choose public campus locations: canteen, library, department corridor. Avoid isolated spots, especially after dark.

    Safety Advisory
  • R06

    Report — don't retaliate

    If someone violates these standards or makes you uncomfortable, use the platform's report function or email the admin. Don't engage in counter-harassment. Let the system handle it — it will.

    Best Practice
  • R07

    Be the senior you wish you had

    Mentor, guide, help — don't intimidate. If you ever feel the urge to "test" a junior through humiliation or pressure, ask yourself: is this the kind of IET graduate you want to be known as?
